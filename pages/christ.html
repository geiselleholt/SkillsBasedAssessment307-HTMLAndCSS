Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ at the summit of Mount Corcovado, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It is a 30 meters tall statue of Jesus Christ, made of soapstone and reinforced concrete. The statue is a major cultural attraction emblematic of Rio and has been named one of the New7Wonders of the World. It was constructed between 1922 and 1931. The statue is an Art Deco statue created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot.

It was funded almost entirely by donations from the Brazilian people

If there's one thing the people of Rio are good at, it's coming together for a shared cause — just look at how Carnival is celebrated in the city each year. Christ the Redeemer's construction cost around $250,000 — the equivalent of $3.4 million today — and was funded almost entirely by the local Catholic community.

It's frequently struck by lighting

Christ the Redeemer is struck by lightning an average of three to six times per year. Its location on a mountaintop within a rainforest makes it particularly susceptible to strikes. In 2014, a lightning strike damaged a few of the statue's fingers and its head, prompting more lighting rods to be added to the statue's head and arms.
