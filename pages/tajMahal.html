Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic marble mausoleum located in the city of Agra, India. It was built between 1631 and 1654 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan as a memorial for his favorite wife, Empress Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The architecture is a blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles.

The Taj Mahal's color changes constantly throughout the day

The shimmering white marble used in the construction of the Taj Mahal changes color according to the time of day — from the uplifting yellow of sunrise to the desolate deep blue of night. People have imagined poetically that the color changes reflect the feelings the late emperor underwent during his time with Mumtaz Mahal and after her death.

28 types of jewels were used

If you step inside, you can find many beautiful tulips, lilies, irises, poppies, and narcissus inlaid into the wall. They are elegantly decorated using pietra dura (a jigsaw-like inlay technique). To make these plants lifelike, 28 types of precious and colorful stones were used, such as orange carnelian, multi-colored agates and chalcedonies, blue lapis lazuli, bloodstone, and garnet.
